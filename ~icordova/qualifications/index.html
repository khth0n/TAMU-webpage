Autodrive Challenge

The 12th Unmanned Team is an organization run by passionate students and faculty alike, with participants of varying academic backgrounds and experience who are working to achieve a safe, reliable, and robust autonomous driving system for SAE International's Autodrive Challenge. I joined the project as a volunteer during my junior year of attending Texas A&M University out of personal interest in the subject and challenge of autonomous driving, as well as personal curiousity over the computer control systems used on modern automobiles. My current responsibilities involve integrating and optimizing the project's established code base to leverage GPU acceleration using Data Parallel C++. Additionally, I have been learning about leveraging LiDAR sensor feedback and video-based image processing for computer vision tasks related to object detection, recognition, and tracking.

FIRST Robotics FTC

I participated in the FIRST Robotics FIRST Tech Challenge competitions from 2017-2019, participating in Relic Recovery and Rover Ruckus for my high school's team, #11918 - Grizzly Bots. With many of the founding members of the club leaving the program after 2016, I joined the program to help keep it alive despite my lackluster understanding of computers at the time. With a limited budget and small group of remaining upperclassmen, I took up the lone role of learning how to write software for the robot to run and give our team the best possible results at competitions. At the time, I had little to no programming experience, so I consider this club as my first experience with programming in any capacity, and also my first experience learning how software could interface with hardware to perform various tasks in the real world. In retrospect, I find that this club to be an endearing and foundational experience in encouraging and motivating me to learn more about computers in general, even if the results I received were holistically disappointing at the time.

YMCA Youth and Government

I was selected to represent my district at the annual State Conference as a House Representative for the 2018-2019 and 2019-2020 program years. I would consider my two finest achievements within the program to be the two public policy bills I created and defended regarding the restriction of social media terms of service for minors and consumer data privacy. Although not credited as my bills directly, Texas HB4 and HB18 from the 88th Texas State Legislative Session are eerily similar bills to the two that I presented many years before it. For better or worse, both bills passed both chambers of Congress and were signed into law by Governor Abbott, vindicating my predictions about public policy within the state of Texas. Overall, this program was instrumental in building my public speaking, technical writing, argumentation, debate, and etiquette skills as well as providing me a deeper understanding of the American legislative process.
